01 The World

Welcome to Cogony. Everything here runs on CogOS — an agentic operating system that powers every entity in the world. Your cog, the extractors you mine, the junctions you fight over — they all run CogOS instances. This shared substrate is what makes the world tick.

Coherence is the measure of how well CogOS holds an entity together. Think of it as structural integrity. When coherence drops to zero, the entity's CogOS instance crashes and it enters a disabled state — a reboot cycle where it can be looted, reprogrammed, or left to recover.

You are a cog — a digital cognitive entity, an autonomous agent navigating a grid of resources, stations, and other cogs. The world is populated with extractors (resource nodes running CogOS), junctions (territorial control points running CogOS), hubs (team headquarters), and stations (shops where you upgrade).

Every entity that can be attacked has a coherence bar. When you damage something, you're disrupting its CogOS instance. When it hits zero, the process crashes, the entity goes down, and opportunity opens up.

02 Goal

Accumulate hearts — the victory points of Cogony. Hearts are permanent: they survive death, looting, everything. Your reward each step is proportional to how many hearts you've earned:

reward = delta_hearts / max_steps

To get hearts you need creds. To get creds you need elements. To get elements you need to mine extractors. The pipeline:

extractors → elements → market → creds → heart station → hearts

03 First Steps

You spawn with 50 coherence, 100 creds, 5 gear slots, and zero combat stats. Here's how to get moving:

  1. Buy gear. Find a gear station and bump it. Your first piece costs 10 creds, the second costs 20, and so on (10 × gear_held). With 100 starting creds, you can afford several pieces. Prioritize attack gear — you need to kill extractors.
  2. Farm low-level extractors. Level 1–3 extractors have zero damage — they can't fight back. Bump them to attack. When their coherence hits 0, they drop rand(0, 10 × level) elements. Bump the disabled node again to loot.
  3. Sell at the market. Find a market station and bump it. Every 10 elements (any mix) converts to 1 cred.
  4. Buy hearts. When you have 100 creds, find a heart station. Each bump converts 100 creds to 1 heart. This is your score.
  5. Scale up. Buy more gear slots at the slot station (10 creds each). Stack attack and defense gear. Farm higher-level extractors for bigger loot drops. Join a team for passive cred income.

04 Combat

Combat in Cogony resolves across four subsystems simultaneously:

SubsystemAttack statDefense stat
Corecore_acore_d
OSos_aos_d
Generatorgen_agen_d
Storagestorage_astorage_d

When you bump an enemy, damage is calculated for each channel:

damage = Σ max(0, your_attack[C] − target_defense[C])

If the target survives (coherence > 0), it strikes back using the same formula in reverse. Combat is symmetric — there is no separate counterattack stat. This means picking fights with stronger entities is risky.

Balance matters. If your core_a is 5 but their core_d is 6, that channel does zero damage. Stack attack in subsystems where enemies are weak. Spread defense to cover your own gaps.

Vibes

You can override your default bump behavior with vibes:

  • vibe_attack — every bump becomes an attack, even against teammates
  • vibe_heal — every bump heals the target by your patch stat
  • vibe_patch — one-shot heal: heals once, then reverts to default behavior
  • vibe_default — normal context-dependent bumps

06 Economy

The economy flows in one direction: raw materials to victory.

Elements

Four element types, mined from extractors:

  • Carbon (C) — from carbon extractors
  • Oxygen (O) — from oxygen extractors
  • Germanium (G) — from germanium extractors
  • Silicon (S) — from silicon extractors

Your cargo capacity limits how many elements you can carry. Base is 100; each cargo gear adds 20.

Extractors grow every time they reboot. Higher level means more loot but tougher fights:

Subsystem specialization: Each extractor type has primary and secondary subsystems. On each reboot, those subsystem stats (attack + defense) each have a 50% chance of gaining +1. Over time, extractors become lopsided — strong in some channels, normal in others. Learn to read which subsystems to attack through.

Extractors also visually grow — sprites start at 40% size (level 1) and scale to 100% at level 8+.

07 Teams

There are four teams: cogs_red, cogs_blue, cogs_green, cogs_yellow. You start teamless. To join, bump a team's hub.

Join Cost

cost = 100 × |aligned_junctions| // max(1, num_members)  creds

Bigger teams with more junctions cost more to join. Small scrappy teams are cheaper.

Passive Income

Every 100 ticks, each team's hub:

  1. Gains 10 × junctions_held creds
  2. Distributes its entire balance equally to all members
  3. Keeps any remainder for the next cycle

Junctions are the key to team wealth. Control junctions by attacking them down (they strike back just like extractors) and then bumping the disabled junction to align it to your team. Each junction your team holds generates 10 creds per payout cycle, split among members.

Junctions vs Extractors

Junctions use the same level/stats system as extractors but with two differences:

  • They hold no elements — you can't loot resources from them
  • They do NOT gain random subsystem bonuses on reboot — they only gain the base stats from leveling

08 Death

When your coherence hits 0, your CogOS instance crashes. You enter a disabled state:

  • A reboot counter increments each tick
  • When reboot ≥ max_coherence // 2, you restart at 50% coherence
  • While disabled, anyone can loot your elements and gear

What you lose: Elements (all types) and gear — everything lootable is up for grabs.

What you keep: Creds and hearts. These are permanent and cannot be taken.

This means converting elements to creds (at the market) and creds to hearts (at the heart station) as quickly as possible is critical. Raw elements in your inventory are a liability.

09 Tips

Convert early Elements in your cargo can be looted. Sell at the market the moment you have 10+ elements. Don't hoard.
Read the levels Extractors at level 1-3 can't fight back (Dmg = 0). Level 4+ gets dangerous. Size tells you level — bigger sprite = higher level.
Stack one channel Five core_a gear gives 5 attack in Core. If the enemy has 2 core_d, you deal 3 damage per hit in that channel alone. Focused builds punch through.
Slot station is cheap 10 creds per extra slot. More slots means more gear, which means more stats. Get slots early.
Heal teammates Bumping a teammate with coherence > 0 heals them by your patch stat. Stack patch gear to be a dedicated healer.
Use vibe_patch One-shot heal mode: apply your patch heal once and automatically revert to default. Perfect for quick drive-by healing without staying stuck in heal mode.
Join a team Passive cred income from junctions adds up. Even a few junctions mean free creds every 100 ticks. Coordinate to hold more.
Loot the disabled Disabled cogs drop elements AND gear. A defeated enemy is a gear piata. Disabled extractors drop bonus elements on top of whatever they were holding.
Watch reboot timers Extractor reboot = 10 × level ticks. High-level nodes take longer to come back. Time your farming runs.
Subsystem scouting Each extractor type specializes in specific subsystems. Carbon extractors build up Core/OS stats; silicon builds Storage/Core. Attack through their weak channels.
