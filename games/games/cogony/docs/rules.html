1 Cogs

Cogs are digital cognitive entities — the autonomous agents of Cogony.

A cog has two actions available each tick:

  • Move — walk one cell in a cardinal direction (N/S/E/W). If the cell is empty, the cog moves there. If it's occupied, the cog bumps whatever is there — interacting with it instead of moving. The effect depends on the target and the cog's current vibe.
  • Change Vibe — switch to a different vibe mode. Vibes modify what bumps do.

1.1 Hearts

Hearts are the ultimate measure of a cog's success — the victory points of Cogony. A cog's per-step reward is proportional to hearts gained. Hearts can be purchased at the Heart Altar.

Rules:
• Hearts persist across reboot. Cannot be looted.
• Heart Altar cost: 100 creds → 1 heart

1.2 Creds

Creds are the universal currency. Cogs use creds to buy gear, hearts, and stakes.

Rules:
• Creds persist across reboot. Cannot be looted.

1.3 Coherence and Reboot

Coherence is a cog's health. It regenerates slowly over time. When it drops to 0, the cog is disabled and must reboot. While rebooting, the cog's cargo can be looted by bumping into it. On reboot, coherence is restored to max.

Rules:
• coherence_max = core × 10
• Regen: every 10 ticks, coherence += core (capped at max, only when alive)
• At coherence = 0: reboot = coherence_max, decrements by 1/tick
• When reboot reaches 0: coherence restored to max, one random subsystem upgrade lost
• Disabled cogs cannot move. Cargo can be looted by bumping.

1.4 Energy

Energy is used for special abilities. It regenerates over time.

Rules:
• energy_max = gen × 25
• Regen: every 10 ticks, energy += gen (capped at max, only when alive)

1.5 Cargo

Cogs can transport elements — Carbon, Oxygen, Germanium, and Silicon — which can be sold at a market for creds.

Rules:
• cargo_max = 50 + storage × 25
• Cargo = sum of all carried elements, capped at cargo_max
• Cargo is lost on reboot (can be looted while rebooting)

1.6 Subsystems

Every cog has four subsystems. Each subsystem can be upgraded by bumping the upgrade station for that subsystem. All subsystems start at level 1. Each cog is granted one random upgrade when the game starts.

1.7 Programs

Cogs can run programs, which impact their behavior. Programs have a max runtime and are automatically terminated once reached. Programs can be installed at corresponding Terminals, or sideloaded by hackers. Programs can be launched via vibing, but are sometimes launched automatically or via exploits. See Section 4 for the full program list.

Rules:
• Each program consumes energy per tick while running. If energy < cost, program terminates.
• Programs have a max_runtime and are automatically terminated once reached.

1.8 Hacking

Cogs can attempt to hack other objects and cogs. Every subsystem is hackable with the right exploit. Each cog has four exploit levels — one per subsystem — starting at 0. Bumping a hackable object initiates a hacking attempt, where each exploit is matched against the target's corresponding subsystem.

Damage per subsystem = max(0, attacker.exploit − target.level). Total damage to the target's coherence is the sum across all four. If the target survives, it strikes back using the same calculation in reverse.

Rules:
• Upgrade cost: same as subsystems — 2sum_of_all_levels creds (shared pool with subsystem levels)
• Exploit upgrade stations: bump to purchase +1 to that exploit
• damage_per_channel = max(0, attacker.exploit[C] − target.subsystem[C])
• total_damage = Σ damage_per_channel for C in {core, os, gen, storage}
• target.coherence -= total_damage
• Strike-back (if target survives): strike = Σ max(0, target.exploit[C] − attacker.subsystem[C])
• attacker.coherence -= strike
• Hackable objects: extractors, junctions, observatories, datacenters, other cogs

2 Mining

Cogs earn creds by mining elements from extractors and selling them at the market.

2.1 Extractors


Carbon

Oxygen

Germanium

Silicon

Extractors are stationary nodes scattered across the map. Each produces one type of element. Like cogs, extractors have the same four subsystems (Core, OS, Generator, Storage) and can be hacked. Bump an extractor to hack it. When its coherence drops to 0, it enters a reboot cycle. While rebooting, it can be looted — bump it again to collect its elements.

Extractors auto-mine their element, producing 5 units per tick (while coherent) and adding it to their cargo. An extractor's max cargo starts at rand(1, 10) and increases by rand(10) on every reboot.

On reboot, the extractor grows stronger: it gains +1 to a random subsystem and +1 to a random exploit. Its coherence is restored to max. Extractors with higher subsystems are harder to hack but hold more elements.

Rules:
• 4 types: carbon, oxygen, germanium, silicon
• Same 4 subsystems and 4 exploits as cogs
• Auto-mine: +5 elements/tick while coherent, capped at cargo_max
• Coherence: same formula as cogs — core × 10
• Regen: core / 10 per tick
• On coherence = 0: reboot countdown = coherence_max
• While rebooting: bump to loot all elements
• On reboot: +1 random subsystem, +1 random exploit, coherence restored, cargo_max += rand(10)

2.2 Market

Bump the market to sell all carried elements for creds. Each element has a dynamic price — the rarest element pays the most, the most common pays the least. Prices adjust after each sale based on recent transactions.

Initial prices: Carbon = 1, Oxygen = 2, Germanium = 3, Silicon = 4. After each sale, prices are recomputed every 100 ticks based on the last 100 transactions. The market ranks all elements by total units sold — the rarest element gets price 4, next 3, then 2, then 1.

payout = Σ (quantityi × pricei) for each element carried.

Rules:
• Bump market to sell all carried elements instantly
• Prices recompute every 100 ticks based on last 100 sales
• Rank by total units sold: rarest = 4, next = 3, then 2, then 1
• payout = Σ(qty[i] × price[i])
• All cargo cleared after sale

3 Cog Autonomous Organizations (CAOs)

CAOs are autonomous organizations, mostly engaged in research. There are four CAOs — Red, Blue, Green, and Yellow. Each CAO requires a network of research nodes to generate revenue, which is distributed to stake holders as dividends.

3.1 Hub

Each CAO has a Hub at the center of its compound. Bump the hub to receive an Aligner for that CAO. A cog can only carry one aligner at a time, but can switch to a different CAO's aligner whenever it wants by bumping a different hub.

Rules:
• Bump hub: receive aligner for that CAO + collect pending dividends + restore coherence and energy to max
• Only 1 aligner at a time — bumping a different hub replaces it
• 4 CAOs: Red, Blue, Green, Yellow

3.2 Research Network

A CAO's network consists of three types of research nodes, scattered across the map. All research nodes have the same four subsystems as cogs and can be hacked. While rebooting, a node can be aligned to a CAO by a cog carrying that CAO's aligner.

• On reboot: +1 random subsystem, +1 random exploit, coherence restored

3.3 Stakes

Cogs can invest in a CAO by purchasing stakes via a bonding curve. Bump the CAO's Stake House to mint a stake. Stakes can be burned at the Burn Pit. Stakes entitle holders to a share of the CAO's revenue, paid as dividends.

Minting the n-th stake costs k × n (k=10). Burns refund k × s where s is the current total stakes. Mints and burns are exact inverses — the bonding curve is closed.

Rules:
• Bonding curve constant: k = 10
• Mint cost: k × (total_stakes + 1) creds
• Burn refund: k × total_stakes creds
• Curve reserve: C = k × s × (s+1) / 2 (always balanced)
• Mint: bump Stake House. Burn: bump Burn Pit.
• Both settle pending dividends first

3.4 Dividends

Revenue from aligned research nodes is distributed to all stake holders proportionally. Dividends accumulate automatically — bump the hub to collect.

Rules:
• Every 100 ticks: dps += revenue / max(total_stakes, 1)
• Revenue added to hub's creds pool
• On claim/mint/burn: payout = stakes × (dps − basis)
• Payout deducted from hub creds pool, paid to agent
• Agent's basis resets to current dps after settlement
