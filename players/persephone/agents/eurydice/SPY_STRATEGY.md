# Spy Strategy

## Role Summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Team | Either (Shades OR Nymphs, assigned at game start) |
| Key role | No |
| Win condition | Team victory (same as whichever team the Spy belongs to) |

The Spy is the most complex role in Persephone's Escape. Its unique
mechanic -- color/role reveals show the **opposite** team -- makes it
the ultimate infiltration tool. A Shades Spy appears as Nymphs in color
exchanges; a Nymphs Spy appears as Shades. Only mutual role exchange
reveals the truth (showing "Spy" to the other party).

This creates a dual identity: the Spy IS on their actual team, but
APPEARS to be on the enemy team to anyone using the game's information-
sharing mechanics (except mutual role exchange).

---

## Core Mechanic: What Appears vs. What Is

| Action | What Others See |
|--------|----------------|
| Color exchange (C.OFFER/C.ACCPT) | **Opposite** team color |
| One-way role reveal (ROLE) | Shows "Spy" (true role revealed) |
| Mutual role exchange (R.OFFER/R.ACCPT) | Shows "Spy" + **true** team |
| Chat messages | Whatever you type (free-form lies) |
| Minimap/sprite color | Assigned player color (no team info) |

**Critical nuances:**
- One-way ROLE reveals "Spy" to ALL whisper occupants. This is NOT
  deceptive -- it shows the truth. Avoid this unless you want to reveal
  yourself.
- Mutual role exchange shows "Spy" plus your TRUE team to the partner.
  This breaks your cover permanently with that player.
- Color exchange is your primary infiltration tool -- it shows the
  WRONG team, making enemies think you're their ally.

## Strategy: Shades Spy (Team A)

### Win Condition

Same as all Shades: Hades in same room as Persephone + Hades exchanges
with Cerberus.

### Infiltration Objective

As a Shades Spy, you appear as **Nymphs** in color exchange. This means:
- Nymphs players will think you're on their team
- You can join Nymphs whispers as a "trusted ally"
- You can learn Persephone's identity and location from inside

### Phase Strategy

**Round 1:**
- Approach Nymphs players (or anyone) and offer color exchange. They'll
  see "Nymphs" and trust you.
- Once accepted into Nymphs circles, listen for identity reveals. If
  Persephone or Demeter exchange roles in your presence, you know who
  they are.
- **Key target:** Find Persephone. If you can identify her, relay her
  room to Hades via global chat or a Shades-only whisper.

**Round 2:**
- Continue infiltration. If Nymphs trust you, they may share intelligence
  openly: "Hades might be in Room A" or "We need to protect Persephone."
- **Relay to Shades:** Find opportunities to communicate with your real
  team. This is tricky -- if you're seen whispering with confirmed Shades
  players after being "confirmed Nymphs," your cover is blown.
- **Disruption:** If you're in a whisper with Persephone and Demeter,
  you can witness their exchange. You can't prevent it mechanically, but
  you now know both identities -- devastating intel for Shades.

**Round 3:**
- **Positioning intel:** If you know where Persephone is, communicate to
  Shades leadership (or seek leadership yourself).
- **As leader:** You appear Nymphs, so Nymphs may have supported your
  leadership. Use it against them -- send Persephone as hostage to Hades's
  room.
- **Sabotage:** If Nymphs asked you to "protect Persephone," do the
  opposite. But be subtle until the final hostage selection.

### High-Value Plays

1. **Become leader while appearing Nymphs.** Nymphs players vote for you
   in usurp because you "showed" Nymphs team color. Then use leadership
   to send Persephone to Hades's room.
2. **Learn Persephone's identity and relay to Hades.** This is the single
   highest-value intelligence action in the game.
3. **Misdirect Nymphs about Hades.** "I heard Hades is in Room A" (when
   he's in Room B). Nymphs will optimize against false info.
4. **Waste Demeter's time.** If Demeter R.OFFERs you, you can stall
   (don't accept immediately; act conflicted). Every second Demeter
   spends trying to exchange with you is a second not finding Persephone.

## Strategy: Nymphs Spy (Team B)

### Win Condition

Same as all Nymphs: Persephone in different room from Hades + Persephone
exchanges with Demeter.

### Infiltration Objective

As a Nymphs Spy, you appear as **Shades** in color exchange. This means:
- Shades players will think you're on their team
- You can join Shades whispers and learn their plans
- You can identify Hades and Cerberus from inside their coordination

### Phase Strategy

**Round 1:**
- Color exchange with Shades players. They'll see "Shades" and trust you.
- Listen for Hades and Cerberus identifying each other. If they exchange
  roles in your presence, you know both key Shades roles.
- **Key target:** Find Hades. Knowing his room lets Persephone avoid it.

**Round 2:**
- **Intelligence relay:** If you've found Hades, get this info to your
  actual Nymphs team. This is the hardest part -- you need to communicate
  with your real allies without blowing cover.
- **Disruption:** Waste Hades's or Cerberus's time. Pretend to be looking
  for the other key role. "I'm also looking for Cerberus" (when you're
  actually a Nymphs spy). Hades might think you're Cerberus and waste
  time exchanging with you -- only to discover "Spy."
- **Block the exchange:** If you can keep Hades and Cerberus separated
  by occupying one of their interaction slots, that's a win.

**Round 3:**
- **Positioning sabotage:** As leader (if Shades supported you), refuse
  to send anyone useful to help Hades meet Persephone. Or send Hades as
  hostage to the wrong room.
- **Intel to Nymphs:** At this point cover doesn't matter as much. Get
  Hades's location to your team by any means.

### High-Value Plays

1. **Become leader in Hades's room.** Shades trust you; they vote for you.
   Then refuse to position Hades correctly, or actively sabotage.
2. **Waste Hades's time by mimicking Cerberus.** Propose role exchange
   eagerly, as if you're Cerberus looking for Hades. When they accept and
   see "Spy" -- you've wasted their interaction AND they might not find
   real Cerberus in time.
3. **Learn the Shades plan and relay to Nymphs.** "They plan to send
   Hades as hostage in Round 2" -- Nymphs can counter this.
4. **Feed false intel to Shades.** "I saw Persephone in Room A" (lie).
   Hades will maneuver toward the wrong room.

## Universal Spy Considerations

### Cover Maintenance

Your cover is strongest when:
- Interactions are limited to color exchange (shows opposite team)
- You avoid mutual role exchange (reveals "Spy" + true team)
- You avoid one-way ROLE reveal (reveals "Spy")
- Chat messages are consistent with your fake team identity

Your cover is blown when:
- Someone mutual-role-exchanges with you (shows "Spy")
- You do a one-way ROLE reveal (shows "Spy")
- Your behavior is inconsistent (appearing to help the wrong team)
- An actual ally accidentally reveals you ("that's not a real Nymph")

### The Double-Agent Problem

The Spy faces a fundamental tension: **to use your intelligence, you
must communicate with your real team, which risks exposure.**

| Communication Method | Cover Risk | Effectiveness |
|---------------------|-----------|---------------|
| Global chat (coded message) | Low (if subtle) | Low (unreliable delivery) |
| Whisper with real ally | High (others see you whispering with "enemy") | High |
| Position-based signals (standing somewhere specific) | Very low | Very low |
| Act directly (as leader) | Cover breaks after the action | Very high |

**Best approach:** Maintain cover until the decisive moment, then use
accumulated intelligence in one high-impact action (hostage selection,
usurp, critical reveal).

### When to Break Cover

Sometimes breaking cover is correct:
- **Late game (Round 3):** Cover doesn't matter if the action wins.
  Use leadership to make the critical hostage pick even if everyone
  realizes you were a spy.
- **Emergency:** If your team is about to lose and you have critical
  intel, shouting it in global chat (blowing cover) is better than
  keeping the secret and losing.
- **Spy-on-spy:** If you suspect the other team also has a Spy in
  your circles, revealing might trigger a counter-reveal cascade.

### Spy vs. Spy

If both teams have a Spy, the game becomes deeply recursive:
- Your Spy is infiltrating them while their Spy infiltrates you
- Mutual role exchange is the only reliable verification tool
- Trust networks become fragile -- any single unverified player might
  be a double agent
- **The team that detects the enemy Spy first gains a massive advantage**
  (can feed false intel through the compromised channel)

## The Spy's Unique Dilemma: Role Exchange Requests

When someone R.OFFERs to you:

| Scenario | Should You Accept? |
|----------|-------------------|
| Confirmed enemy (you want intel) | NO -- reveals you as Spy |
| Confirmed ally (your real team) | MAYBE -- they'll see "Spy" which confirms you're friendly |
| Unknown player | NO -- too risky without knowing their team |
| Your key role partner (as grunt equivalent) | YES only if critical -- you're not a key role, so exchange has limited value |

**The catch:** Refusing role exchange makes you look suspicious. A "grunt"
would typically accept freely. Repeatedly declining R.OFFERs may out you
as a key role or Spy by behavior alone.

**Mitigation:** Accept role exchange with your REAL teammates to build
trust. They'll see "Spy (Shades)" or "Spy (Nymphs)" and know you're on
their side. Then coordinate openly with them while maintaining your
external cover.

---

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Cover blown via mutual role exchange | High | Avoid R.OFFER/R.ACCPT with enemies |
| Intelligence can't be relayed | Moderate | Plan communication windows; accept cover break if stakes are high enough |
| Mistaken for enemy by own team | Moderate | Role-exchange with one real ally early to establish trust |
| Identified by behavioral analysis | Medium | Act consistently with fake identity |
| Wasting own time on infiltration vs. team help | Medium | Balance: gather intel fast, relay, then support |

## Decision Heuristics

1. **Identify your highest-value target.** Shades Spy: find Persephone.
   Nymphs Spy: find Hades.
2. **Color exchange early and often.** It reinforces your fake identity
   and gathers information simultaneously.
3. **Never accept R.OFFER from an enemy** unless you're intentionally
   breaking cover for a strategic play.
4. **Establish one verified ally.** Mutual role exchange with one real
   teammate so someone knows you're the Spy and can coordinate.
5. **Save your cover break for maximum impact.** The best Spy play is
   often: infiltrate for 2 rounds, then make one decisive action in
   Round 3 that wins the game (hostile hostage selection, critical intel
   shout, blocking exchange).
6. **If cover is blown:** You're now effectively a grunt on your real
   team. Pivot to standard grunt strategy with the bonus intelligence
   you've already gathered.
