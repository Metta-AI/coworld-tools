# Persephone Strategy

## Role Summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Team | Nymphs |
| Key role | Yes |
| Win condition | End in a **different room** from Hades AND complete mutual role exchange with Demeter |

Persephone is the mirror of Hades but with a crucial asymmetry: her
positional goal is **separation** rather than co-location. This is
strategically easier to achieve defensively (stay put, resist being
moved) but harder to guarantee (she can be moved against her will via
hostage selection). The exchange with Demeter is mechanically identical
to the Hades-Cerberus pair.

---

## Objectives (Priority Order)

1. **Find and exchange roles with Demeter.** Hard mechanical requirement.
   Without it, Nymphs cannot win.
2. **Determine Hades's location.** Critical for the separation condition.
   If you know where Hades is, you know which room to avoid.
3. **Maintain separation from Hades.** Stay in a different room. Resist
   hostage selection. Seek or influence leadership.
4. **Protect own identity.** If Shades know where Persephone is, they
   can engineer co-location via hostage exchange.

---

## The Defensive Advantage

Persephone's separation condition is inherently **easier to satisfy than
Hades's co-location condition**, assuming neither side has perfect
information:

- If neither side knows where the other is, the probability of being
  in different rooms at game end is 50% (random).
- Persephone only needs to NOT be moved to Hades's room. Hades needs to
  actively GET to Persephone's room.
- **Holding position is simpler than forcing movement.** Persephone wins
  the positional game by doing nothing, as long as Shades can't identify
  and move her.

This means identity protection is Persephone's strongest positional tool.

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy

### Roster Reveal

- **Memorize room composition.** Note player sprites in both rooms.
- **Identify your roommates.** You'll interact with these players; start
  mentally categorizing them.

### Round 1 (Find Demeter, Protect Identity)

**Priority: Locate Demeter while minimizing exposure.**

- **Selective whisper creation.** Don't broadcast your eagerness. Create
  whispers with 1-2 players max in Round 1.
- **Color exchange as safe probe.** C.OFFER reveals team without exposing
  your specific role. Use it to find Nymphs teammates.
- **With confirmed Nymphs:** Propose mutual role exchange. If they're
  Demeter -- critical objective achieved. If they're a grunt, enlist
  them for intelligence and protection.
- **With confirmed Shades:** Be cautious. Extract what you can ("have
  you found your key roles?") without revealing yourself. Then exit.
  Don't linger in whispers with potential enemies.
- **Global chat:** Observe the shout strip. Don't send messages that
  reveal your room or identity unless communicating with confirmed allies.
- **Passive position:** If Demeter might be in the other room, you may
  need to send signals -- but avoid anything Shades can use to locate you.

### Round 2 (Exchange and Fortify)

**If Demeter is found and in your room:**
- Execute mutual role exchange immediately. Highest priority.
- Then shift to defensive posture: protect your room assignment.

**If Demeter is in the other room:**
- You have a dilemma: you need the exchange, but changing rooms risks
  co-location with Hades.
- **Preferred solution:** Get Demeter sent to you as hostage. Coordinate
  via global chat or through grunt relays.
- **Risky alternative:** Go to Demeter's room as hostage. Only do this
  if you're confident Hades is NOT in that room.
- **Intelligence is critical here.** You need to know Hades's room before
  deciding whether to move.

**Identity defense:**
- If Shades are actively probing (lots of whisper requests), be selective
  about who you interact with.
- Grant whisper entry cautiously. Prefer interactions with confirmed allies.

### Round 3 (Hold Position)

**Priority: Stay in a different room from Hades.**

- **If exchange is done and you know Hades's room:** Simply don't be
  in that room. Resist hostage selection. Seek leadership.
- **If exchange is done but Hades's location is unknown:** Monitor
  global chat for intelligence. Assume defensive posture -- staying put
  has a 50% base rate of success.
- **If exchange is NOT done:** This is a crisis. Same urgency as Hades
  facing the same situation -- the exchange must happen or the game is
  lost.
- **Leadership is defensive gold.** If you're leader, you cannot be
  selected as hostage. This guarantees your room position.

### Leader Summit

- **If you're leader:** You're safe from being moved. Use the summit to
  gather intel on the other room. Probe the other leader for Hades
  information.
- **Pass leadership carefully.** If you pass leadership, you become
  hostage-eligible. Only pass to a trusted Nymphs ally.

### Hostage Exchange

- **Key question:** Is Hades being moved to your room, or are you being
  moved to Hades's room? You have no control here -- the outcome depends
  on leadership decisions already made.
- **Post-exchange assessment:** If you've been moved, immediately
  reassess whether you're now in Hades's room. Adjust Round 3 strategy
  accordingly.

## Interaction Strategies

### With Demeter (Critical Partner)

- Mechanical interaction: mutual role exchange. Identical to
  Hades-Cerberus.
- **Recognition:** Demeter will be a Nymphs player eager for role
  exchange. Any Nymphs teammate who proposes mutual role exchange
  quickly is likely Demeter (grunts are less urgent).
- **Post-exchange coordination:** Demeter can serve as your intelligence
  relay. They know your identity and can protect you in conversations
  with others.

### With Nymph Grunts (Allies)

- **Intelligence network.** They can safely probe Shades players to find
  Hades (via color/role exchange), then relay the room information.
- **Bodyguards.** An ally in a whisper with you makes it less likely
  you'll be alone with a Shades player who might identify you.
- **Leadership candidates.** A Nymph grunt as leader can protect you
  from hostage selection.
- **Decoys.** They can claim to be Persephone in global chat to misdirect
  Shades' targeting.

### With Hades/Cerberus (If Identified)

- **High danger.** If Hades identifies you, he knows exactly which room
  to target.
- **Avoid mutual role exchange with Shades key roles.** It gives them
  confirmation of your identity and location.
- **Color exchange is modestly safe** -- it reveals Nymphs team, which
  narrows you to Persephone or Demeter or Nymph. Not immediately
  dangerous but still informative.
- **Exit quickly.** Don't linger in whispers with identified Shades
  key roles.

### With Shades Grunts

- **Moderate danger.** They'll relay your identity/location to Hades.
- **Possible intelligence source.** They might inadvertently reveal
  Hades's location ("Hades already found Cerberus" implies both are in
  the grunt's room).
- **Waste their time.** If a Shade grunt is in a whisper with you,
  they're not helping Hades.

### With Unknown Players

- Color exchange first. Sort friend from foe.
- Be more cautious than Hades would be -- your identity is worth more
  defensively than offensively.

## Defensive Tactics

### Identity Protection

| Threat | Defense |
|--------|---------|
| Color exchange reveals Nymphs | Low risk (doesn't reveal specific role) |
| One-way role reveal | NEVER do this outside confirmed all-Nymphs whisper |
| Mutual role exchange with enemy | NEVER -- confirms identity + location |
| Chat messages | Never say "I'm Persephone" in mixed company |
| Behavioral inference | Don't act "special" -- behave like a grunt would |

### Anti-Hostage Measures

- **Seek leadership.** Leaders cannot be hostaged.
- **Ally as leader.** Communicate with the leader (if Nymphs) that you
  should NOT be sent.
- **Usurp enemy leaders.** If the leader is Shades and might send you,
  coordinate a majority usurp.
- **Signal to allies:** "DON'T SEND ME" can be communicated via whisper
  to the leader if they're an ally.

### Counter-Intelligence

- **If Shades are probing you:** Give false signals. Claim to be Demeter
  (less valuable target). Or claim to be a grunt (boring, not worth
  pursuing).
- **Room ambiguity:** If you suspect you've been identified, spreading
  false room information in global chat can buy time.
- **Behavioral camouflage:** Act like a grunt -- probe players yourself,
  show team color freely. The more "normal" you appear, the less Shades
  will target you specifically.

## Persephone vs. Hades: The Information War

The game is fundamentally asymmetric:

| Factor | Persephone (Defense) | Hades (Offense) |
|--------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Goal | Stay apart | Get together |
| Info need | Where is Hades? | Where is Persephone? |
| Position control | Resist being moved | Force movement |
| Identity value | Hiding wins | Finding wins |
| Default outcome | Random 50% helps defense | Random 50% hurts offense |

**Persephone wins the information war by not playing it.** If neither
side knows where the other is, the 50/50 random room split favors
Persephone (separation is the default state). Hades must actively break
this equilibrium.

---

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Identified by Shades | High | Behavioral camouflage; claim to be grunt |
| Sent to Hades's room as hostage | Critical | Seek leadership; lobby allies; usurp |
| Can't find Demeter | Critical | Use grunt network; global chat signals |
| Spy on Nymphs | Medium | Mutual role exchange reveals truth |
| Time pressure for exchange | High | Prioritize Demeter search in Round 1 |

---

## Decision Heuristics

1. **Demeter first, defense second.** The exchange is mandatory; defense
   alone can't win.
2. **Never role-exchange with confirmed Shades.** The information cost
   is catastrophic.
3. **If exchange is done:** Shift to pure defense. Seek leadership,
   stay in your room, resist all movement.
4. **If you must change rooms for the exchange:** Verify Hades is NOT
   in the destination room first. Use grunt intelligence.
5. **When in doubt about moving:** Stay put. The default state favors
   separation.
6. **Leadership is your strongest defensive tool.** If you're leader,
   you're immune to hostage selection and you control who leaves.
