# Cerberus Strategy

## Role Summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Team | Shades |
| Key role | Yes |
| Win condition | Hades must complete mutual role exchange with Cerberus (you) for Shades to win |

Cerberus is the **enabler** of the Shades' win condition. Without
Cerberus completing the mutual role exchange with Hades, the Shades
cannot win -- period. This makes Cerberus's primary mission clear and
singular: find Hades and execute the exchange. Unlike Hades, Cerberus
has no positional requirement, which gives more flexibility in movement
and hostage decisions.

---

## Objectives (Priority Order)

1. **Find Hades and complete the mutual role exchange.** The single most
   important action for the Shades team.
2. **Facilitate Hades-Persephone co-location.** Once the exchange is done,
   help position Hades in the same room as Persephone.
3. **Gather intelligence on Persephone's location.** Relay to Hades or
   use leadership to arrange favorable hostage exchanges.
4. **Protect Hades's identity.** Don't inadvertently reveal which player
   is Hades to Nymphs.

---

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy

### Roster Reveal

- **Note room composition.** You'll need to identify who in your room
  might be Hades. In a 5-person room with ~2-3 Shades members, Hades
  has a ~40-60% chance of being in your starting room.
- **If split from Hades:** You may need to engineer a hostage move.
  Start planning contingencies.

### Round 1 (Find Hades)

**Priority: Identify teammates and locate Hades.**

- **Initiate whispers aggressively.** Time is short; you need to probe
  as many players as possible.
- **Color exchange first.** Use C.OFFER to quickly sort Shades from
  Nymphs. This costs minimal time and reveals team affiliation.
- **With confirmed Shades:** Immediately offer mutual role exchange.
  If they're Hades -- mission accomplished. If they're a grunt --
  enlist them to help find Hades or relay your location.
- **Broadcasting availability:** Consider a global chat message like
  "SHADES MEET AT [coordinate]" -- risky (Nymphs can read it) but it
  accelerates team coordination. Only do this if time pressure demands it.
- **Don't waste time with Nymphs.** Once a color exchange reveals a
  Nymph, extract yourself and move to the next target. You can gather
  intel on Persephone's identity later.

### Round 2 (Execute or Reposition)

**If Hades is found and in your room:**
- Execute mutual role exchange immediately. This is a single whisper
  interaction: one player R.OFFERs, the other R.ACCPTs. Takes ~4-6
  seconds if both are in the whisper.

**If Hades is in the other room:**
- **Volunteer as hostage.** This is your best move. Communicate with
  your room's leader (or seek leadership yourself) to get sent across.
- **Coordinate via global chat.** A message like "SEND ME" or a
  pre-arranged signal to allies in the other room.
- **If you're leader:** You cannot be a hostage. Consider passing
  leadership to a grunt, THEN being available for selection.

**If Hades hasn't been found:**
- Continue whisper-probing players. Prioritize anyone you haven't
  interacted with yet.
- Use global chat to signal: you exist and you're looking. Hades
  should be looking for you too.

### Round 3 (Final Push)

- **If exchange is NOT done:** This is the last chance. Every second
  counts. If Hades is in your room, sprint to them. If not, you need
  the hostage exchange to bring you together.
- **If exchange IS done:** Shift to support mode. Help position Hades
  near Persephone. Use leadership, hostage lobbying, or intelligence
  sharing.
- **Sacrifice play:** If the exchange is done and positioning is the
  issue, you can volunteer as hostage without concern -- your mechanical
  role is complete. Getting Hades to Persephone's room (or vice versa)
  is all that matters now.

### Leader Summit

- **If you're leader and exchange is done:** Use the summit to probe
  the other leader about their room composition. Can you identify
  Persephone's room from their behavior?
- **If you're leader and exchange is NOT done:** Consider passing
  leadership during the summit to the other leader if they're a Shades
  ally (unlikely in standard play). More realistically, use the summit
  to coordinate hostage picks that bring you and Hades together.

## Interaction Strategies

### With Hades (Critical Partner)

- The interaction is mechanical once found: mutual role exchange.
- **Recognition signals:** Hades will be a Shades player who is
  cautious about revealing their specific role. If a Shades-confirmed
  player immediately offers role exchange, they're likely Hades or
  Cerberus (a grunt would be less urgent).
- **Mirror strategy:** Since both Hades and Cerberus are looking for
  each other, a Shades player who seems eager for mutual role exchange
  is likely your partner.

### With Shade Grunts (Allies)

- Confirm team via color exchange, then role-exchange to verify they're
  a grunt (not Hades). This information is still valuable.
- Enlist them as messengers: "Tell Hades I'm looking" via shout.
- Ask them to seek leadership for favorable hostage selection.
- They can safely reveal team affiliation to help coordinate.

### With Nymphs Players

- **Low priority for interaction.** Your main job is finding Hades.
- **Intelligence extraction:** If time permits, probe for Persephone's
  location. A Nymph who's chatty about their room composition might
  leak useful info.
- **Avoid revealing you're Cerberus.** If Nymphs know who Cerberus is,
  they can try to prevent the Hades-Cerberus meeting (by getting you
  hostage-selected away, or targeting you for usurp/disruption).
- **Deception:** Claim to be a grunt or even a Nymph in chat. Your
  specific identity is less critical to protect than Hades's, but
  revealing it still helps the enemy.

## Cerberus vs. Hades: Asymmetric Roles

While both need the same exchange, their strategic situations differ:

| Factor | Hades | Cerberus |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Positional requirement | Must be in same room as Persephone | None |
| Risk of being identified | Very high (enemy primary target) | Moderate |
| Flexibility for hostage moves | Limited (room matters) | High (room doesn't matter) |
| Post-exchange value | Must stay near Persephone | Freed to support |

This asymmetry means Cerberus should be the **more mobile** partner.
If anyone needs to cross rooms via hostage exchange, it should be
Cerberus rather than Hades (unless Hades also needs to reposition
toward Persephone).

## The Urgency Calculus

The exchange MUST happen before the final hostage exchange concludes.
With 3 rounds of 15 seconds each, total playing time is 45 seconds.
Budget:

| Action | Time Cost |
|--------|-----------|
| Walk to a player | 2-4 seconds |
| Create/enter whisper | 1-2 seconds |
| Color exchange | 3-4 seconds |
| Role exchange | 3-4 seconds |
| Exit whisper | 1 second |

A full probe cycle (approach + whisper + color exchange + exit) costs
~8-10 seconds. With 15-second rounds, you get 1-2 interactions per
round. In 3 rounds, that's 3-6 players probed. In a 5-person room
(with you as one), you have 4 candidates -- manageable if efficient.

**Implication:** Don't waste interactions on players already confirmed
as non-Hades. Keep a mental list and be systematic.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Can't find Hades (different room) | Critical | Volunteer as hostage; use global chat signals |
| Time runs out without exchange | Critical | Prioritize speed over caution |
| Spy impersonating Hades | Medium | Mutual role exchange reveals truth -- if they're a Spy, you'll know |
| Identified by Nymphs | Moderate | Limits usefulness for post-exchange support |
| Selected as hostage away from Hades | High | Communicate with leader; seek leadership yourself |

---

## Decision Heuristics

1. **Every interaction should answer: "Is this Hades?"** If it can't
   advance that question, skip it.
2. **If you confirm someone is Shades via color exchange:** Immediately
   propose mutual role exchange. Don't waste a round being polite.
3. **If Hades is in the other room and it's Round 2+:** Volunteer as
   hostage. Signal your intent via global chat or ally relay.
4. **If exchange is done:** You're free. Maximum value now comes from
   intelligence-gathering on Persephone's location and relaying it to
   Hades.
5. **If it's Round 3 and exchange is NOT done:** Accept any risk to
   make the exchange happen. Reveal identity in shout if it helps
   coordinate. The game is lost without the exchange regardless.
