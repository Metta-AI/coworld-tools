# Nymph (Grunt) Strategy

## Role Summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Team | Nymphs |
| Key role | No |
| Win condition | Nymphs team wins (Persephone in different room from Hades AND Persephone exchanges with Demeter) |

The Nymph grunt is the defensive counterpart to the Shade grunt. Where
Shades grunts enable offense (helping Hades find and reach Persephone),
Nymph grunts enable defense (protecting Persephone's location and
blocking Hades's positioning). The fundamental asymmetry: **defense
scales better than offense** -- one well-placed Nymph leader can block
Shades' entire positional strategy.

---

## Objectives (Priority Order)

1. **Facilitate the Persephone-Demeter exchange.** Help them find each
   other without exposing Persephone to Shades.
2. **Locate Hades.** Knowing where Hades is lets Persephone avoid that
   room. This is the single highest-value intelligence.
3. **Protect Persephone's identity and location.** Act as a shield,
   decoy, or misdirection for Shades trying to locate her.
4. **Control leadership for hostage defense.** Prevent Persephone from
   being sent to Hades's room.
5. **Disrupt Shades coordination.** Interfere with the Hades-Cerberus
   meeting if possible.

---

## The Defensive Multiplier

Nymph grunts are more valuable than they appear because defense has
inherent advantages in this game:

- **Default state favors Nymphs.** Persephone starts in a random room;
  50% chance she's already separated from Hades. Nymphs just need to
  maintain the status quo.
- **One blocker > one attacker.** A Nymph leader who refuses to send
  Persephone as hostage negates ALL of Shades' positioning efforts.
- **Information asymmetry favors defenders.** Shades must FIND
  Persephone (affirmative action); Nymphs just need to prevent that
  discovery (denial).
- **Decoys are free.** Any Nymph grunt can claim to be Persephone,
  creating false positives that waste Shades' limited investigation time.

---

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy

### Roster Reveal

- **Standard room memorization.**
- **Count and assess.** With 5 Nymphs total (Persephone, Demeter, 3
  grunts), your room likely has 2-3 Nymphs allies.

### Round 1 (Intelligence and Identification)

**Priority: Find teammates, locate Hades, set up defensive network.**

- **Color exchange with everyone.** Sort the room into teams as fast as
  possible. This information is immediately actionable.
- **With confirmed Nymphs:** Mutual role exchange to identify who's who.
  Are they Persephone? Demeter? Fellow grunt? This builds your internal
  team map.
- **With confirmed Shades:** This is your intelligence opportunity.
  - Probe for Hades. A Shades player who refuses role exchange might be
    a key role (grunts have no reason to refuse).
  - Observe behavior: a Shades player urgently seeking other Shades is
    likely Hades or Cerberus trying to find their partner.
  - Note: anyone who proposes mutual role exchange and you see "Shade"
    is a grunt -- harmless. Someone who AVOIDS role exchange is suspicious.
- **Global chat monitoring:** Watch the shout strip. Shades signals to
  each other may reveal positioning intentions.

### Round 2 (Protect and Disrupt)

**Priority: Ensure Persephone-Demeter meeting; block Shades positioning.**

- **If Persephone and Demeter are both in your room:** Stand guard.
  Don't let Shades interrupt their exchange. If you're leader, don't
  send either of them as hostage.
- **If one key role is missing:** Help arrange the right hostage move.
  If you're leader, consider sending a Shades player OUT to make room
  for an incoming ally.
- **Active defense as leader:** If you're leader and Persephone is in
  your room, NEVER send her. Select Shades players as hostages instead.
- **Disruption play:** Engage Shades key roles in time-wasting whispers.
  If you can keep Hades or Cerberus talking to you for 10 of the 15
  seconds in a round, that's 10 seconds they can't exchange with each
  other.
- **Decoy duty:** In global chat, claim "PERSEPHONE IS HERE" (referring
  to your room) even if she isn't. Shades will try to maneuver toward
  a phantom target.

### Round 3 (Final Defense)

- **If you're leader:** This is the decisive moment. Your hostage pick
  determines final positions.
  - If Persephone is in your room and Hades is in the other: send
    anyone EXCEPT Persephone. She stays safe.
  - If Hades is in your room: consider sending Hades as hostage (puts
    him in the other room, away from Persephone... unless Persephone is
    already there. Intel is critical).
- **Usurp hostile leaders.** If the leader is Shades and might send
  Persephone to Hades's room, coordinate with other Nymphs for a
  majority usurp. This must happen BEFORE hostage selection phase.
- **Volunteer as hostage** if it helps the team. Example: if you going
  to the other room displaces a Shades player who was feeding intel
  to their team.

## Interaction Strategies

### With Persephone (If Found)

- **Protect her.** Don't reveal her identity in global chat or to
  Shades players.
- **Relay Hades intel.** If you've found Hades, tell Persephone which
  room he's in so she can avoid it.
- **Assist the exchange.** If Demeter is in the other room, help
  coordinate the hostage move to bring them together.

### With Demeter (If Found)

- **Same as Persephone interaction.** Help them find each other.
- **If they're in different rooms:** Help arrange movement. Demeter
  is more mobile than Persephone (no positional requirement), so
  usually Demeter should cross to Persephone.

### With Fellow Nymph Grunts

- **Coordinate roles:**
  - One seeks leadership (hostage defense)
  - One probes Shades for Hades intel
  - One acts as decoy in global chat
- **Usurp coalition:** If leader is hostile, you need 3+ votes in a
  5-person room. Two Nymph grunts + one ally can usurp.
- **Split investigation targets** to maximize coverage.

### With Shade Grunts (Enemies)

- **Intelligence extraction.** Shade grunts might accidentally reveal
  Hades's location ("I've already talked to everyone in my room" --
  implies they know who's there).
- **Misdirection feed.** Tell them Persephone is in a specific location
  (false). They'll relay it to Hades, who'll optimize against wrong info.
- **Time wasting.** Keep them in whispers as long as possible. Offer
  false alliance, pretend to negotiate, ask lots of questions.

### With Hades/Cerberus (If Identified)

- **Critical intelligence.** Knowing Hades's room is the most valuable
  single fact for the Nymphs team.
- **How to identify Hades:** A Shades player who:
  - Refuses color exchange (protecting identity)
  - Refuses or avoids mutual role exchange
  - Is actively seeking specific Shades teammates
  - Asks probing questions about player identities
- **Don't tip your hand.** If you identify Hades, don't let him know
  you know. Quietly relay to allies via separate whisper or cryptic
  global chat.
- **Block their meeting.** If you know where both Hades and Cerberus
  are, use leadership to keep them separated (send one as hostage in
  the wrong direction).

## The Decoy Strategy

Nymph grunts can safely impersonate Persephone because:
- In chat: lies cost nothing. Claim "I'm Persephone" freely.
- In color exchange: you show Nymphs team color, same as Persephone.
- In one-way ROLE reveal: shows "Nymph" -- so DON'T do this while
  impersonating (it breaks your cover). Avoid role reveals if playing decoy.
- In mutual role exchange: shows "Nymph" -- reveals the bluff. Only
  do this with allies.

**Optimal decoy play:**
1. In global chat: "PERSEPHONE HERE IN [your room]"
2. Accept whisper requests from Shades players who come investigate
3. In the whisper: stall, ask questions, waste their time
4. Eventually they'll demand role exchange -- at this point you've
   burned 10+ seconds of their round
5. The reveal that you're "just a Nymph" is fine -- the damage is done

## Leadership Strategy

Nymph grunts as leaders are **pure defense:**

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Persephone in your room | Never send her. Send a Shades player or yourself. |
| Hades in your room | Send Hades (separates him from Persephone if she's elsewhere) |
| Neither key role in your room | Send strategically (Shades player out) |
| You're forced to pick a Nymph | Pick yourself (grunt sacrifice) or another grunt |

**Seeking leadership:**
- If randomly assigned: excellent. Use it.
- If not: consider whether usurp is worth the political capital.
  Best deployed when current leader is Shades and likely to make
  hostile picks.
- Pass leadership to Persephone if she wants it (grants her immunity).

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Accidentally revealing Persephone's identity | Critical | Never mention her by name unless in Nymphs-only whisper |
| Hostile leader sends Persephone to Hades | Critical | Usurp before hostage select |
| Wasting time on irrelevant interactions | Moderate | Prioritize: Hades intel > team coordination > disruption |
| Being identified as decoy | Low | Costs nothing; the time waste already happened |
| Spy infiltrating Nymphs communications | Moderate | Verify via mutual role exchange before sharing critical intel |

---

## Decision Heuristics

1. **"What does Persephone need right now?"** -- This is your compass.
   Every action should serve her safety or her exchange.
2. **Hades intel is gold.** If you can figure out which room Hades is in,
   that single fact may win the game.
3. **Leadership > everything** in Round 3. If you can control hostage
   selection in the final round, you control the outcome.
4. **Time-waste Shades whenever possible.** Their win condition requires
   active coordination; yours requires preventing it.
5. **When in doubt, be loud.** Nymphs benefit from chaos and confusion.
   Shades benefit from clarity and organization. Disrupt their signals.
