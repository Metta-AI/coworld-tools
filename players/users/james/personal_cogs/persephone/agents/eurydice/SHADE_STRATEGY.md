# Shade (Grunt) Strategy

## Role Summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Team | Shades |
| Key role | No |
| Win condition | Shades team wins (Hades in same room as Persephone AND Hades exchanges with Cerberus) |

The Shade grunt has no personal mechanical requirement -- no exchange to
complete, no positional constraint. This apparent weakness is actually
freedom: Shades grunts are the **most flexible operatives** on the team.
They can take risks that key roles cannot, serve as intelligence relays,
seek leadership, and act as decoys or distractions.

---

## Objectives (Priority Order)

1. **Facilitate the Hades-Cerberus exchange.** Help them find each other.
   Relay information. Create conditions for their meeting.
2. **Locate Persephone.** Knowing which room she's in is critical for
   the positioning win condition.
3. **Control or influence hostage selection.** Seek leadership to move
   pieces favorably, or lobby the current leader.
4. **Protect key role identities.** Don't leak Hades's location to
   Nymphs. Actively spread misinformation.
5. **Disrupt Nymphs coordination.** Prevent the Persephone-Demeter
   exchange if possible; waste Nymphs players' time.

---

## The Grunt Advantage

As a grunt, you have unique freedoms:

- **Safe to role-exchange with anyone.** Revealing "Shade" is low-cost.
  It tells Nymphs nothing actionable. Use this to quickly verify
  identities.
- **Safe to reveal team.** Color exchange is risk-free for you. Use it
  liberally.
- **Expendable for hostage moves.** You can volunteer as hostage with
  no strategic cost to your team -- your room doesn't matter.
- **Safe to claim any identity.** You can impersonate Hades or Cerberus
  in chat to confuse Nymphs, with no risk of contradiction via mechanical
  reveals (since you can choose not to role-exchange with enemies).
- **Leadership is pure upside.** If randomly selected as leader, you can
  directly control hostage picks without the complication of being a key
  role.

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy

### Roster Reveal

- **Memorize room layout.** Note all player sprites/colors for later
  reference.
- **Probability assessment.** In a standard 10-player game with 5 Shades
  (Hades, Cerberus, 3 grunts) and 5 Nymphs (Persephone, Demeter, 3
  grunts), your room likely has 2-3 teammates.

### Round 1 (Scout and Identify)

**Priority: Map the team composition in your room.**

- **Rapid color exchanges.** Approach each player in sequence and
  C.OFFER. You want to know friend from foe as fast as possible.
- **With confirmed Shades:** Mutual role exchange to learn if they're
  Hades, Cerberus, or fellow grunt. If Hades or Cerberus, ask if they've
  found their partner yet.
- **With confirmed Nymphs:** Probe for information. Ask about room
  composition in the other room. Try to identify Persephone or Demeter.
  Consider one-way ROLE reveal to build false trust (you're "just a
  grunt," which makes them think you're harmless).
- **Report via global chat.** If you find something useful (e.g., "no
  key shades roles in this room"), relay via shout strip -- but be
  cryptic enough that Nymphs can't easily parse it.

### Round 2 (Coordinate and Disrupt)

**Priority: Enable key roles; disrupt enemy coordination.**

- **If Hades and Cerberus are both in your room:** Protect them. Don't
  let Nymphs interfere with their whisper. If you're leader, don't
  send either of them as hostage.
- **If one is missing:** Help arrange the hostage exchange. If you're
  leader, try to select a Nymphs player as hostage (to make room for
  incoming allies) or communicate with the other room's leader.
- **Active disruption:** Enter whispers with Nymphs players and waste
  their time. Offer fake alliance, ask irrelevant questions, pretend
  to negotiate. Every second a Nymph spends with you is a second they're
  not finding Demeter/Persephone.
- **Impersonation:** Claim to be Cerberus in whispers with Nymphs --
  this makes them think Cerberus is in your room and potentially
  misdirects their hostage strategy.

### Round 3 (Final Moves)

- **If you're leader:** This is your highest-value moment. Select
  hostages to position Hades near Persephone (if you know where both
  are) or to bring Cerberus to Hades (if exchange isn't done).
- **Volunteer as hostage** if your being moved helps the team.
  Example: if Hades needs to be in the other room and the leader is
  Nymphs, volunteer to go -- you can't help the positioning, but you
  reduce the Nymphs information network in that room.
- **Usurp an unhelpful leader.** If the current leader is Nymphs or
  a confused ally, coordinate with other Shades to usurp via majority
  vote before hostage selection.

### Leader Summit

- **If you're leader:** Use the summit to probe the opposing leader.
  Learn room composition. Negotiate (honestly or deceptively) about
  hostage swaps.
- **Pass leadership strategically.** If a key role ally needs leadership
  (unlikely but possible), you can pass it during a whisper.

## Interaction Strategies

### With Hades (If Found)

- Confirm identity via mutual role exchange (safe for you).
- Ask what they need: "Have you found Cerberus?" "Do you know where
  Persephone is?"
- Relay any intelligence you've gathered.
- Protect their identity -- don't mention their role in global chat
  or mixed whispers.

### With Cerberus (If Found)

- Same as Hades interaction. Confirm via exchange, ask about status.
- If Cerberus needs to get to Hades's room, help arrange it.

### With Fellow Grunts

- Coordinate roles: "You take leadership, I'll scout the other room"
  or "Let's both vote to usurp."
- Split investigation targets -- don't both probe the same players.
- Establish a communication relay if key roles are in different rooms.

### With Nymphs Grunts

- **Extraction targets.** They might know where Persephone or Demeter
  is. Probe via conversation.
- **Waste their time.** Extended whisper conversations mean they can't
  help their team.
- **Spread misinformation.** "Hades is in Room B" when he's in Room A.
  Nymphs will optimize hostage selection around wrong info.

### With Persephone/Demeter (If Identified)

- **Critical intelligence.** Knowing which room Persephone is in is
  the team's second win condition.
- **Don't reveal you know.** If you identify Persephone, don't let
  her know you've identified her -- she'll be more cautious about
  staying hidden.
- **Relay to Hades** immediately via global chat or a Shades-only
  whisper.
- **Disruption:** If you're in a whisper with both Persephone and
  Demeter, your presence blocks their exchange (whisper occupant
  witnesses). Actually no -- they CAN still exchange with you present.
  But you can waste their time and report their activities.

## Leadership Strategy

Grunts make excellent leaders because:
- No personal positional requirement to conflict with team needs
- Safe from being hostage-selected (leaders are exempt)
- Full control over who gets sent where
- Can pass leadership to an ally when appropriate

**When leader:**
1. Identify team composition in your room
2. Never send Hades as hostage (unless Hades needs to go to Persephone)
3. Prefer sending Nymphs as hostages (weakens their info network locally)
4. If you know Cerberus is in the other room, try to arrange a swap

**When not leader:**
- If leader is Nymphs, coordinate usurp with allies
- If leader is an uninformed Shades ally, whisper with them and share intel
- Lobby for specific hostage picks via global chat

## Deception Playbook

| Deception | Target | Effect |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| "I'm Hades" | Nymphs in whisper | Nymphs think Hades is in your room; may misallocate |
| "I'm Cerberus" | Nymphs in whisper | Nymphs think key Shades pair might be in same room |
| "Hades is in [wrong room]" | Global chat | Nymphs optimize positioning against false info |
| Offer role exchange to Nymphs | Any Nymph | Wastes their time; reveals you're "just a Shade" (harmless) but also uses up their interaction slot |
| "I haven't found anyone yet" | Any | Conceals your knowledge from enemies |

**Key principle:** As a grunt, being caught in a lie costs you nothing.
You have no critical secret. The worst that happens is enemies stop
trusting you -- which means they stop wasting time on you.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Accidentally revealing Hades's identity | High | Never mention Hades by name in mixed company |
| Being manipulated by a Spy | Medium | Mutual role exchange reveals truth |
| Wasting too much time on useless probes | Medium | Be systematic; track who you've talked to |
| Leader is unhelpful and resistant to usurp | Moderate | Need ally coordination for majority |

---

## Decision Heuristics

1. **No interaction? Move.** As a grunt, you should always be doing
   something -- probing, communicating, seeking leadership.
2. **Prioritize unknown players over known ones.** Once you've confirmed
   someone's team, move to the next target.
3. **Relay intel immediately.** Information decays fast in a 15-second
   round. Global chat is visible to all, but timeliness matters more
   than secrecy for room composition intel.
4. **If all local players are identified:** Volunteer as hostage to
   scout the other room.
5. **If it's Round 3 and the exchange isn't done:** Your only job is
   helping Hades and Cerberus meet. Everything else is noise.
