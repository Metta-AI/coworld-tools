# Hades Strategy

## Role Summary

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Team | Shades |
| Key role | Yes |
| Win condition | End in **same room** as Persephone AND complete mutual role exchange with Cerberus |

Hades has the most complex win condition in the game: it requires both a
**positional outcome** (same room as Persephone) and a **mechanical action**
(R.OFFER/R.ACCPT with Cerberus). These two requirements create tension --
Hades must coordinate with a teammate (Cerberus) while simultaneously
manipulating an adversary's position (Persephone).

---

## Objectives (Priority Order)

1. **Find and exchange roles with Cerberus.** This is the hard mechanical
   requirement. Without it, Shades cannot win regardless of positioning.
2. **Determine Persephone's location.** Needed to ensure co-location at
   game end.
3. **Ensure co-location with Persephone.** Either move to her room or get
   her moved to yours via hostage exchange.
4. **Protect own identity.** Nymphs gain a major advantage by knowing which
   room Hades is in -- they can simply keep Persephone elsewhere.

---

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy

### Roster Reveal

- **Memorize the room composition.** Note which player sprites/colors are
  in your room vs. the other. This is the only time you see the full roster
  layout.
- **Count players per room.** With 10 players split roughly evenly, you'll
  have 5 per room. Your room has 4 potential allies/enemies to investigate.

### Round 1 (Information Gathering)

**Priority: Find Cerberus.**

- **Approach teammates quickly.** Create whispers with players in your room.
  Use color exchange (C.OFFER) as the first probe -- it reveals team
  affiliation with no risk to your specific role identity.
- **Identify Shades teammates.** Once you confirm a player is Shades via
  color exchange, escalate to role exchange discussion. A Shade grunt will
  typically be willing to role-exchange freely since they have nothing to hide.
- **Distinguish Cerberus from Shade grunts.** If a Shades teammate offers
  mutual role exchange and turns out to be Cerberus -- you've completed the
  critical objective. If they're a grunt, they can still be useful as an
  information relay.
- **Manage time carefully.** 15 seconds is short. Prioritize one meaningful
  interaction per round over scattered partial contacts.
- **Global chat:** Avoid sending messages that reveal your identity or
  location. Observe the shout strip for intelligence from others.

### Round 2 (Execution)

**Priority: Complete the Cerberus exchange if not done; begin positioning.**

- **If Cerberus is found and in your room:** Execute the mutual role exchange
  immediately. This is the highest-priority action in the game.
- **If Cerberus is in the other room:** You need one of:
  - Cerberus volunteered as hostage to come to you
  - A Shades leader in the other room sends Cerberus to you
  - You volunteer as hostage to go to Cerberus (risky -- changes your room)
- **Begin Persephone intelligence.** If the exchange is done, shift focus
  to learning where Persephone is. Shades grunts who've interacted with
  Nymphs players may have intel.
- **Leadership value:** If you're leader, you control hostage selection.
  Don't waste this on random picks -- use it strategically.

### Round 3 (Final Positioning)

**Priority: Ensure same room as Persephone at game end.**

- **If exchange is done and you know Persephone's room:**
  - Same room: Hold position. Resist being hostage-selected out.
  - Different room: You MUST become a hostage (volunteer by approaching
    your leader and communicating), or get Persephone sent to you.
- **If exchange is NOT done:** This is an emergency. You need Cerberus in
  your room before the final exchange. All other concerns are secondary.
- **Hostage selection is the last lever.** After Round 3's hostage exchange,
  positions are final. This is the last chance.

### Leader Summit

- **If you are leader:** You have direct access to the opposing leader.
  This is a high-value interaction -- you can probe their team, offer
  misleading deals, or learn Persephone's room from their perspective.
- **If an ally is leader:** Communicate your needs before the summit
  (via whisper or shout). Tell them who to send/request as hostage.

### Hostage Exchange

- **No input possible.** Use this time to process what you know and plan
  the next round.

---

## Interaction Strategies

### With Cerberus (Teammate - Critical)

- Once identified, the interaction is simple: mutual role exchange.
- If not in the same room, coordinate a meeting point. Use global chat
  cautiously ("meet me" signals) or rely on hostage mechanics.
- **Time is the enemy.** Don't waste rounds on verification theatre --
  once you're confident someone is Cerberus (via color exchange showing
  Shades), propose the role exchange immediately.

### With Shade Grunts (Teammates - Supportive)

- They're your intelligence network. After confirming team via color
  exchange, ask them to:
  - Report Persephone's location if they find it
  - Seek leadership and select hostages favorably
  - Spread misinformation to Nymphs (e.g., claim Hades is in the other room)
- Grunts can safely role-exchange with anyone since it only reveals "Shade"
  -- not dangerous information.

### With Nymphs Players (Adversaries)

- **Never reveal your role.** Persephone knowing where Hades is gives
  Nymphs a decisive positional advantage.
- **Color exchange is risky.** It reveals you as Shades, which narrows
  down that you might be Hades or Cerberus.
- **Deception options:**
  - Claim to be a Nymph grunt in chat (lies are free)
  - Claim to be Cerberus to deflect suspicion from the "Hades" hunt
  - Avoid all interaction with Nymphs entirely (safe but loses intel)
- **Information extraction:** If you do whisper with a Nymph, you can
  probe for Persephone's location indirectly ("who else have you talked to?")

### With Unknown Players

- Start with color exchange. It's the safest information-gathering tool.
- If they're Shades: escalate to role exchange (looking for Cerberus).
- If they're Nymphs: extract what intel you can without revealing yourself.

---

## Deception and Counter-Intelligence

### Identity Protection

- Nymphs' optimal counter-strategy is: "Find Hades, keep Persephone in
  the other room." If they know which room you're in, they win the
  positioning battle easily.
- **Never use one-way ROLE reveal** unless you're certain the audience is
  all Shades.
- **Be cautious with color exchange** -- it narrows you to Hades or
  Cerberus (since grunts don't need to be secretive about team).

### Active Deception

- **Claim to be Shade grunt** in chat. This is the least interesting thing
  for Nymphs to know.
- **Claim to be in the other room** via global chat if it would confuse
  Nymphs' positioning calculations.
- **Impersonate Nymph** in mixed whispers (risky but high reward -- you
  might learn Persephone's location from a trusting Nymph).

### Spy Awareness

- A Spy on your team appears as Nymphs in color exchange. Be aware that
  not everyone showing pink is actually Nymphs.
- A Spy on Nymphs appears as Shades. Someone showing green in a color
  exchange might be an enemy Spy -- verify via mutual role exchange if
  uncertain. Mutual role exchange always reveals truth.

---

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| Identity revealed to Nymphs | High | Avoid role reveals; limit color exchanges |
| Can't find Cerberus | Critical | Use grunts as scouts; volunteer as hostage if needed |
| Sent to wrong room as hostage | High | Seek leadership; communicate with allies |
| Time runs out without exchange | Critical | Prioritize Cerberus search above all else |
| Spy impersonating Cerberus | Medium | Only mutual role exchange reveals truth |

---

## Decision Heuristics

1. **If Cerberus is in your room and exchange not done:** Nothing else
   matters. Get into a whisper with them and execute R.OFFER/R.ACCPT.
2. **If exchange is done and Persephone's room is unknown:** Gather
   intelligence. Color-exchange with unknowns; ask grunts what they know.
3. **If exchange is done and Persephone is in the other room:** You need
   to either go there (hostage) or get her sent here (ally as leader).
4. **If it's Round 3 and exchange is NOT done:** Panic mode. If Cerberus
   is unreachable, the game is likely lost. Focus entirely on the exchange.
5. **If you are leader:** Use hostage selection to advance positioning.
   Select a Nymphs player from your room if you want to reduce their
   info network, or select yourself if you need to change rooms (note:
   leaders cannot be selected as hostages, so you'd need to pass
   leadership first).
